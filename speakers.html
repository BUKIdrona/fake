QKD Based Fiber Connect 2024

August 9th - 10th, 2024 | NSUT, New Delhi

Invited Speakers

Prof. Abhishek Dixit

(IIT Delhi)

Title of the Talk (Tentative): To be announced

Short bio: Dr. Abhishek Dixit, a distinguished researcher at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi, specializes in integrated circuits (ICs). These microchips form the foundation of modern electronics, from everyday devices to powerful computers. Dr. Dixit’s work focuses on modelling and characterizing CMOS devices, addressing challenges related to static variability, aging, and radiation effects. His collaborations with industry ensure optimal IC performance, contributing to advancements in quantum computation, RF-CMOS technology, and reliability studies

Prof. Anirban Pathak

Title of the Talk (Tentative): To be announced

Short bio: Prof. Anirban Pathak, a distinguished theoretical physicist, obtained his Ph.D. from Visva-Bharati, Santiniketan, India. Following this, he conducted post-doctoral research at Freie University, Berlin. Since 2002, he has been a faculty member at JIIT, Noida. His current focus lies in teaching and research related to quantum optics and quantum information, with a specific emphasis on quantum cryptography. Dr. Pathak is a fellow of prestigious organizations such as The National Academy of Sciences, India (NASI), Institute of Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering (IETE), and Optical Society of India (OSI). He leads a research group dedicated to quantum optics and quantum information, collaborating actively with research groups both in India and abroad. His contributions extend to completed and ongoing projects funded by DST and DRDO. In recognition of his work, he received the 2017 Shri O. P. Bhasin Award in Electronics and Information Technology.

Prof. Neel Kanth Kundu

Title of the Talk (Tentative): To be announced

Short bio: Dr. Neel Kanth Kundu, currently an Assistant Professor at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi, holds expertise in the field of electronic and computer engineering. His academic journey includes a B.Tech. degree in electrical engineering from IIT Delhi and a Ph.D. from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), specializing in scientific computation. Dr. Kundu’s research interests span signal processing for 6G wireless communications, quantum communications, sensing, and quantum information processing. His contributions extend to completed and ongoing projects funded by DST and DRDO. Dr. Kundu has received prestigious awards, including the DST INSPIRE Young Faculty Fellowship, and has actively collaborated with research groups both in India and abroad.

Prof. Abhijit Mitra

Title of the Talk (Tentative): To be announced

Short bio: Dr. Abhijit Mitra, a seasoned researcher, obtained his Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi (2017) under the prestigious British Telecom Fellowship. His expertise lies in modelling and network planning for metro and core optical networks. Dr. Mitra has an impressive publication record in renowned journals such as the Journal of Optical Communication and Networking (JOCN) and the Journal of Lightwave Technology (JLT). He has also contributed to major conferences like the Proceedings of the IEEE, Optical Fiber Communications (OFC) Conference, and the European Conference on Optical Communications (ECOC). His collaborative efforts extend to funded projects by DST, MIETY, and DRDO. Dr. Mitra’s accolades include the DST Inspire Faculty Fellowship, British Council Professional Achievement Awards, and prestigious fellowships such as the Fulbright Post Doctoral Research Fellowship and Sparkle-Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) Cofund Fellowship. With a decade of research experience in Transport Optical Networks, he continues to make significant contributions to the field.

Dr. Atul Kumar Gupta

Title of the Talk (Tentative): To be announced

Short bio: Atul Kumar Gupta has more than 30 Years of experience in the design and development of Optical Communication based products. He has worked on design of Synchronous Digital Hierarchy (SDH) based systems, Fiber Access System, Optical Amplifiers, Giga-bit capable Passive Optical Network (GPON), Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM) equipment, Quantum key Distribution (QKD) etc. He holds B.E. (Electronics and Communication) from DCE, Delhi and MTech. (Opto-electronics and Optical Communication) from IIT, Delhi and is currently designated as Head (Optical Technologies) at C-DOT.

Dr. L. Venkata Subramaniam

Title of the Talk (Tentative): To be announced

Short bio: Mr. Venkata Subramaniam is the IBM Quantum India Leader. His present goal is to set up Quantum Computing as a leading area of research in India. He has contributed/led 6 products, 34 patents, 150 papers and 3000 citations.
